Reaching Outwards, Growing Minds, Building Futures

What we mean by a grounded education

Three commitments sit behind every lesson plan, every report card, and every conversation we have with a parent.

Academic Discipline

A structured curriculum with clear standards at every grade, regular assessment, and teachers who know exactly where each child stands and what comes next.

Individual Attention

Class sizes kept deliberately small, so a child who is struggling is noticed in week one, not in the third term, and a child who is ready can be stretched further.

Character Formation

Manners, responsibility, and respect are taught as deliberately as mathematics, through a daily routine that gives children structure they can rely on.

Academic Programme

Three phases, one continuous path

Full Curriculum Guide
Ages 3 to 5

Pre-Primary

Reception and Grade 0 build the habits that carry a child through school: listening, following instructions, holding a pencil correctly, and working alongside classmates.

  • Phonics and early literacy
  • Number sense and counting
  • Fine motor and handwriting readiness
Grade 1 to 3

Junior Primary

Reading, writing, and arithmetic form the core of Junior Primary, with a focus on building a positive, can-do mindset and the habits of independent, accountable learning.

  • Daily reading and comprehension
  • Weekly mental mathematics
  • Introduction to Afrikaans or German
Grade 4 to 7

Senior Primary

Independence, personal responsibility, and resilience are deliberately developed through Senior Primary, culminating in the Grade 7 Learning for Life Leadership Programme.

  • Natural Science, Social Studies, ICT and AI
  • Formal end-of-term examinations
  • Learning for Life Leadership Programme

Admissions

How a place is secured

Admissions Requirements
01

Enquire

Submit an enquiry form or call the admissions office to confirm space is available for the grade you need.

02

Visit

Tour the campus with the admissions team, meet the relevant grade teacher, and see a typical classroom in session.

03

Assess

Children entering Grade 1 and above complete a short placement assessment in literacy and numeracy.

04

Enrol

Once offered a place, complete enrolment paperwork and registration fee to confirm the seat for the new term.
